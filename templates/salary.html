Average Teacher Salary in Protest States vs. US National Average

Overview

Unlike other professions like doctors, lawyers, or accountants, teacher's contributions to American society are vastyl underappreciated, which leads to chronic undercompensation. This page considers whether the salaries in protest states were below the US National Teacher Salary Average between 1990 - 2016.
States evaluated:

  • US National Avg - Green
  • Arizona - Blue
  • North Carolina - Purple
  • Georgia - Red
  • West Virginia - Yellow
  • Kentucky - Grey
  • Colorado - Orange
  • Oklahoma - Black
  • Virginia - Brown
    •

    Analysis

  • In general, all 8 protest states have average teacher salaries below the US National Average.
  • The one 'failed' strike, Georgia, has the average teacher salary closest to the US National Average.
  • The gap between the US National Average and each protest state's average has increased over the last 30 years.
  • During the past 30 years, the pay gap is largest in Oklahoma. Teachers in Oklahoma made 24.3% less than the US National Average in 1990, and have seen the pay gap increase to 27.8% in 2017.
