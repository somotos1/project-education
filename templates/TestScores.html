8th Grade Test Scores in Protest States vs. US National Average

Overview

One of the arguments made in States that have protested for increases in pay is that low pay or reductions in funding have impacted students’ academic success. This page considers the extent to which students’ test scores (specifically in Mathematics) have declined between 2003 - 2017. Hover over the State names to compare each state to the US National Average. Click the ‘Dollars per Student’ label to compare funding per student over the years.

Analysis

  • Test Scores: In most of the States that protested, Mathematics test scores have seen a decline since 2011. Most dramatic is the 1.84% decline in scores in South Carolina between 2011 and 2017. North Carolina and Oklahoma also witnessed some of the most dramatic declines, with 1.73% and 1.64% declines respectively.
  • Dollars Per Student:The general trend has been an overall increase in funding per student nationwide. However, as can be noted on the chart, all states with protests have received less funding per student than the nationwide average.
